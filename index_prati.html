About the PRATI Test

Developed specifically for Hong Kong Baptist University PRA students, the PRATI assessment helps you discover your unique personality type and career fit.

Scientific Foundation

Built on Jungian psychological type theory and validated through empirical research on PR and advertising professionals' personality traits.

8 Personality Types

Identifies 4 distinct PR personality types and 4 advertising personality types, each with unique strengths and career paths.

Personalized Guidance

Provides tailored career recommendations and development pathways based on your unique personality profile.

Assessment Dimensions

Relationship vs. Creative Focus

Measures your preference for building relationships versus creating innovative content. PR professionals typically focus on relationships, while advertising professionals often emphasize creativity.

Strategic vs. Tactical Thinking

Assesses your approach to problem-solving and planning. Strategic thinkers excel in long-term planning, while tactical thinkers thrive in execution and implementation.

Risk Aversion vs. Risk Taking

Evaluates your comfort with uncertainty and change. PR often requires risk management, while advertising may demand more risk-taking for creative breakthroughs.

Analytical vs. Intuitive Processing

Measures how you process information and make decisions. Analytical thinkers rely on data, while intuitive thinkers trust instincts and patterns.

Development Process

The PRATI assessment was developed through a rigorous process including literature review, expert interviews, pilot testing with HKBU PRA students, and statistical validation of results. The assessment has been designed specifically to address the unique career decision needs of PRA students choosing between PR and advertising specializations.

Research Phase

Comprehensive review of personality psychology literature and career assessment tools, with focus on PR and advertising industry requirements.

Development Phase

Creation of assessment dimensions, question development, and personality type framework based on industry expert input and academic research.

Validation Phase

Pilot testing with HKBU PRA students, statistical analysis of results, and refinement of the assessment based on feedback and data analysis.
